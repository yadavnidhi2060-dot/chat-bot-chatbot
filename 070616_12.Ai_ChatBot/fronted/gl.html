Overview

The Samaritan Law is designed to protect and encourage individuals who assist those in emergencies. It ensures that people acting in good faith to help someone in need are not penalized for unintentional harm caused while providing aid.

Key Point: Acting as a Good Samaritan is both a moral and legal responsibility.

Basic Rules

  • Offer immediate assistance to individuals in emergency situations.
  • Do not expect compensation for helping unless specified by law.
  • Act in good faith and with reasonable care.
  • Do not abandon the person once you begin helping.
  • Contact emergency services promptly.

Government Guidelines

  • Good Samaritans are legally protected from liability when helping in emergencies.
  • Medical professionals should provide aid as per standard protocols.
  • Document the assistance given, if possible, to avoid misunderstandings.
  • Spread awareness about the law in schools, workplaces, and communities.

For more information, visit the official government website or contact local authorities.
