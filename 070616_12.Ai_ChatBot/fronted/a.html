About the Good Samaritan Law

Our Mission

The Good Samaritan Law is designed to encourage bystanders to help road accident victims without fear of legal consequences. Too often, lives are lost because people hesitate to help, fearing harassment from police, hospitals, or courts.

What This Law Ensures

Any person, whether a passerby, driver, or ordinary citizen, can step in to assist accident victims. Helpers are protected from unnecessary questioning, detention, or liability for the victimâ€™s outcome. This legal protection is a step towards saving lives and building a responsible society.

Government Initiative

The Government of India has issued guidelines under the Motor Vehicles Act to protect Good Samaritans. These rules are legally binding, and all hospitals and enforcement authorities are required to comply.
